Michelle Ailport

This year, I hope to show Greenway that being a NHS member is more than just something to put on a college application. NHS signifies more than just 28 hours a year — it’s a commitment to our school, our community, and most importantly, ourselves. National Honor Society members can represent the part of the school that we as a community are most proud of. They can represent the difference between right and wrong. Action and inaction.

And, service and character can mean a variety of things. I’ve learned that it can be totally spontaneous, like picking up a piece of trash on your way to class or holding a door open for someone.

Since I joined my Girl Scout troop in 2008, I have been tirelessly committed to school and community involvement.

Benjamin Jones

The past 3 years of high school have taught me a lot. They have helped me recognize the importance of community, friendship, and leadership. Most importantly, though, they have shown me how these communities and friendships operate.

This year, we will be working hard to expand Greenway’s NHS program to a wider scope than before. With a larger student body, committed officers, and more emphasis on organization, I believe that we can give this group of hard-working students greater opportunities to benefit their communities through service and leadership.

Additionally, I’ve set up this website for this year. If you have any comments/suggestions related to the website, feel free to contact me.

Lee Ann Demers

As officers for this year’s NHS, our goal is to increase our efficiency and organization. This means creating a system that allows members to check their hours regularly, updating members on community service opportunities, and other like actions.

Our goal for the organization as a whole is to prove that NHS is more than its hour and grade requirements — more than the sum of its parts. NHS is a personal commitment to comporting yourself with the values of NHS irrespective of the time or place.

I have the rare privilege of being your NHS officer this year and I hope together we can work to better our community at school and abroad.
